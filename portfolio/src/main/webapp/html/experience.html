The basic premise of CIWA is to prevent the over or under-watering of crop trees across the Central Valley. This helps farmers make more money while helping save water. A value known as the water index is used to show how over or under-watered a tree is. The water index is determined by taking the temperature of the sunlit leaves of the tree. Using a thermal camera can allow taking the temperature of many leaves at once, giving a much more accurate water index than would be possible otherwise. This is the main goal of CIWA. By taking a thermal image of a tree and applying a neural network to pick out sunlit leaves, the average sunlit leaf temperature, and therefore the water index, can be determined extremely quickly. In combination with the historical and current weather data I gather and manage in our database, the crop yield for a tree can be estimated with a high degree of accuracy.
